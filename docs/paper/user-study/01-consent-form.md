## Purpose of the study

You are being invited to participate in a research study evaluating
the usability of a software interface for governing what an AI agent
remembers. The interface lets a human reviewer look at memory writes
the agent has proposed, see a diff against what is already stored, and
approve or reject each proposed write. The goal of the study is to
find out how cognitively demanding the interface is for a software
practitioner using it for the first time, and to find any specific
parts of the interface that are confusing.

## What participation involves

If you agree to participate, you will:

1. Complete an 8-question pre-study survey about your professional
   background (~5 minutes).
2. Join a one-on-one remote session with the researcher on Zoom
   (~40 minutes total). During the session you will:
   - Receive a brief orientation to the interface (~5 minutes).
   - Complete five tasks against a demo database, thinking aloud as you
     work (~15 minutes).
   - Fill out a standard cognitive-workload questionnaire (NASA-TLX,
     ~3 minutes).
   - Answer a few post-task questions about the interface (~5 minutes).
   - Take part in a brief debrief conversation (~5 minutes).
3. Share your screen during the task portion so the researcher can see
   your interactions with the interface.

You will be **video and audio recorded** during the session, with your
screen captured. If you would prefer not to be recorded, the session
can still proceed and the researcher will take written notes â€” please
let the researcher know at the start of the session.

## Voluntary participation and withdrawal

Participation is entirely voluntary. You may decline to answer any
question, skip any task, or end the session at any time, for any
reason, without penalty. If you withdraw before completing the
session, you will still receive the full compensation if you wish, and
all recordings collected up to that point will be deleted within 7
days. Withdrawal will not affect any current or future relationship
with the researcher, the institution, or the memwire open-source project.

## Risks

The risks of participating are minimal. The interface tasks may
occasionally feel frustrating if a task is unclear; please remember
that the interface, not you, is being evaluated. The researcher will
not judge your performance, and there are no "wrong" answers in the
human sense â€” the study is measuring how well the interface conveys
information, not how well you perform.

There is also a small risk that recordings, despite our safeguards,
could be inadvertently disclosed. To mitigate this, recordings are
stored encrypted, accessed only by the study team, and deleted within
90 days of your session.

## Benefits

You will be compensated USD 15 (Amazon gift card or equivalent of your
choice, delivered by email within 48 hours of your session). There is
no direct benefit to you beyond the compensation and the exposure to a
governance-UI design pattern you may find professionally interesting.

The research aims to benefit the broader software-engineering and
AI-safety community by contributing empirical evidence about how
practitioners use HITL governance interfaces for AI agents.

## Confidentiality and data handling

- **No real name is recorded in study data.** You are assigned an
  anonymous code (`P01`, `P02`, ...) at the start of your session.
  All later data uses only that code.
- **What is collected:** your survey responses, NASA-TLX scores,
  Likert ratings, your think-aloud audio + video + screen recording,
  and the researcher's notes.
- **What is NOT collected:** your real name (after recruitment is
  closed), your IP address, your employer's name, or any identifier
  beyond the participant code.
- **Recordings** are stored encrypted on the institution's secure
  cloud storage. They are **deleted within 90 days** of your session
  once transcripts have been verified.
- **De-identified data** (transcripts with names removed, quantitative
  CSV with only participant codes) is retained for 5 years per
  institution policy and may be released as supplementary material with
  the published paper.
- Quoted excerpts from your think-aloud may appear in the paper, but
  only after they have been reviewed to ensure they do not identify
  you or your employer.
